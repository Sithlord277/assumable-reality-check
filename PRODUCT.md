# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Arizona homebuyers who have heard that some homes may have assumable mortgages and want to understand whether that opportunity is real for their situation. They are likely using the product on a phone at a home show, after a conversation, or while researching options, and they need plain-language guidance before deciding whether to talk with Martin Tsatskin.

## Product Purpose

The Assumable Reality Check is a mobile-first guided education tool. It teaches buyers how assumable mortgages work, makes the equity gap concrete, helps users think through timing, cash, loan type, and openness to alternatives, then routes them to the right next-step call with Martin Tsatskin.

Success means a buyer leaves with clearer judgment: not just excitement about a low rate, but an understanding of whether the full deal structure may work for them.

## Brand Personality

Approachable advisor, premium education, plainspoken confidence.

The product should feel like a sharp real estate professional walking a buyer through the numbers without pressure. It can be memorable and crafted, but it should never feel like a gimmick or a finance app trying too hard.

## Anti-references

Avoid dark fintech dashboards, payday-loan energy, childish quiz apps, generic startup abstraction, overly decorative glass effects, and cartoon-like education screens. Avoid UI that makes the topic feel casual in the wrong way; the buyer should feel comfortable, but the money should still feel real.

## Design Principles

1. Make the equity gap impossible to miss.
2. Let Martin's advisor voice lead before visual decoration does.
3. Earn trust through clarity, restraint, and concrete numbers.
4. Use premium moments to improve comprehension, not to distract from it.
5. Keep the flow fast, mobile-first, and low-pressure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast and interaction basics. Support reduced-motion preferences, avoid relying on color alone for meaning, and keep buttons, choices, and financial examples readable on small phone screens.
